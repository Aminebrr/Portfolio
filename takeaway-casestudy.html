01 — The Brief

The problem we addressed.

Takeaway held strong market share in Benelux, but growth was becoming more expensive in France and Germany. Repeat behaviour was not improving at the same pace as acquisition, and the brand was often perceived as functional rather than emotional.

Business Challenge

Repeat-order behaviour had started to plateau in key markets, while acquisition costs were increasing year over year — making retention a more critical growth lever.

Brand Challenge

Takeaway was often perceived as the more price-driven option — efficient, but with lower emotional attachment compared to competitors.

Communication Objective

Build stronger emotional relevance around the idea of craving — positioning Takeaway as a brand that feels present in real-life hunger moments, not only as a delivery service.

Target Audience

Urban millennials and Gen Z (18–34), with a secondary focus on busy professionals. Both segments over-indexed on late-evening orders, social occasions, and convenience-led behaviour.

02 — Strategic Framework

Strategic approach

The strategy focused on making the brand more present in real-life craving moments — not just at standard mealtimes, but in late-night, social, and impulse-driven contexts.

1
🧠
Capture the moment

Focus on high-intent moments rather than fixed mealtimes. Target users based on time of day, weather, and context signals to increase relevance when cravings actually happen.

2
🔁
Reinforce repeat behaviour

Use CRM and loyalty mechanics to reinforce repeat behaviour — turning occasional orders into more consistent habits through reminders, incentives, and low-friction re-engagement.

3
📣
Build cultural presence

Work with creators and platform-native formats to make craving feel relatable and shareable, helping the campaign live within everyday social content rather than only in paid media.

03 — Campaign Execution

12-week rollout plan.

01
Weeks 1–3 · Ignition
Teaser & Brand Heat

Teaser-style 'What are you craving?' placements ran across OOH and social in selected cities, using QR codes to drive users into a lightweight quiz experience before the reveal. Creator seeding helped build curiosity and early conversation around the idea.

Weeks 4–7 · Amplify
Big Brand Moment + Paid Blitz

The main reveal rolled out across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and paid social, supported by multiple creative variations adapted by time of day, weather, and user behaviour. Loyalty messaging was introduced at the same time to connect awareness with repeat-order intent.

Weeks 8–10 · Sustain
Community & Culture Play

Creator-led and community formats helped make the campaign more participative. CRM email and push leaned on previous order behaviour, while retargeting was used to reconnect quiz participants and engaged audiences with stronger conversion messaging.
